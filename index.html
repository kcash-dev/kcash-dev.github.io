about

Hey there! My name is Kyle, I'm a front-end web developer (if you haven't figured that out already) and I'm from the United States. I am still new to web development, but I have a burning curiousity about learning new things and making cool things happen. Before I began my coding journey, I was an English teacher. Many of my ideas for my side projects have come from trying to make my job easier as a teacher. I enjoy doing this using Javascript, but hope to continue learning other technologies to expand my capabilities.

How about something about myself that doesn't include web development, you ask? Well sure! Firstly, I am a staunch hater of brussels sprouts. Other than those, I love almost all foods.I am an avid trail runner. In addition to sports, I enjoy traveling and have lived abroad for the majority of my recent life. While traveling, I enjoy learning the local language the best I can and enjoy making a fool out of myself in front of locals as I try to speak it.

If you would like to know anything else or are interested in working with me (unless you like brussels sprouts), click the button below to contact me.

projects

apps

student tracker

  • Uses: HTML, CSS, Javascript, Firebase/Firestore

  • Designed for freelance teachers to track their students information, progress, payment amount, as well as having the ability to add prior class feedback and video replays.

parts of speech

  • Uses: HTML, CSS, Javascript, and Dictionary API

  • This project was designed to assist educators and educational professionals with the ability to get a words part of speech quickly. By typing in the sentence, the individual will receive the sentence back with the words highlighted according to their part of speech and the key provided below the input box.

workout tracker

  • Uses: HTML, CSS, Javascript, Firebase/Firestore

  • For workout enthusiasts who want to keep a log of their workouts across multiple sports. Athletes have the ability to enter in an exerice, the distance (if applicable), time, and calories burned during the exercise. Athletes workouts are stored in Firestore and then can be accessed to view the athlete's workout history.

websites

garbage miles

  • Uses: HTML, CSS, Javascript

  • This is a personal project for an organization I am working towards creating. The idea is to link running enthusiasts who also have a passion for conservation and protecting the earth. This site is also a template for a blog or organization.

jack's snacks

  • Uses: HTML, CSS, Javascript

convenient food mart

  • This website was designed for a relative's local gas station/convenience store. I worked with the employees to find the target areas and items that they would like to push and emphasized them in the design structure. This also serves as a layout for a small business.
